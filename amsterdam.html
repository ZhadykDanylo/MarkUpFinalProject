1. Pancake Houses: Dutch pancakes, known as pannenkoeken, are a must-try in Amsterdam. The city has numerous pancake houses offering both sweet and savory versions of this delicacy.
  2. Historic Windmills: You can still see working windmills within the city limits of Amsterdam. They were once used for various industrial purposes, including grinding grain and draining water from polders.
  3. Tolerance and Diversity: Amsterdam is celebrated for its progressive and inclusive culture. It was the first city in the world to legalize same-sex marriage in 2001 and is known for its welcoming attitude towards people of all backgrounds.
  4. City of Canals: Amsterdam is known for its extensive canal system, which includes over 100 kilometers of canals, 1,500 bridges, and 90 islands. It's often called the "Venice of the North."
  5. Tulip Mania: In the 17th century, the Dutch experienced "Tulip Mania," where the price of tulip bulbs skyrocketed to extraordinary levels, making them more valuable than houses at the time.
  6. Red Light District: Amsterdam's Red Light District is famous worldwide. Prostitution is legal and regulated in the Netherlands, and the district is known for its distinctive red-lit windows.
